ISO 27001 Surveillance Audit Preparation Checklist

Your comprehensive guide to audit readiness and ISMS compliance

This roadmap ensures your Information Security Management System (ISMS) remains compliant and robust for surveillance audits. The structured checklist covers key tasks to verify scope, policies, risks, and controls, helping your team stay organized and confident. Each task addresses mandatory requirements like security awareness and team competence, demonstrating continuous improvement and audit readiness.

Task Actions Timeline
Verify and Update ISMS Scope Review scope for changes in organization, locations, or assets; update scope document; obtain management approval; communicate changes. 4–6 months before audit
Review and Update Policies/Procedures Identify expired or outdated policies/procedures; update documents (e.g., Information Security Policy, Incident Response); maintain version control; obtain authorization; communicate updates. 4–6 months before audit
Update Key ISMS Records Validate KPI matrix (e.g., incident metrics, training completion); update risk register, objectives register, communication register, and supplier register. 4–6 months before audit
Verify Risk Assessment Activities Conduct risk assessment review for new assets/threats; update risk treatment plans and Statement of Applicability; document evidence (e.g., reports, meeting minutes); test risk response plans. 3–4 months before audit
Review and Validate Annex A Controls Review all 93 Annex A controls (e.g., A.6.2.1 – Competence, A.6.3 – Awareness); verify implementation and records (e.g., training logs, competency records); address gaps; document findings. 3–4 months before audit
Conduct Internal Audit Develop audit plan, prioritizing high-risk areas; perform audit with independent auditors; document findings (nonconformities, improvements); update CAPA register. 2–3 months before audit
Conduct Management Review Meeting Schedule MRM with top management; present scope, policies, risks, controls, and audit findings; document minutes, decisions, and action items. 1–2 months before audit
Final Preparations Review audit plan from certification body; close CAPA items; conduct employee training (e.g., phishing awareness); perform pre-audit dry run; verify third-party compliance; arrange audit logistics; monitor ISO 27001/27002 updates. 1–2 months before audit
Ongoing Tasks Monitor ISO 27001/27002 updates or regulatory changes; maintain centralized evidence repository; document continuous improvements (e.g., new tools). Ongoing

Use this checklist systematically to ensure audit readiness and maintain continuous ISMS improvement. Regular reviews and proactive preparation are key to successful surveillance audits.
